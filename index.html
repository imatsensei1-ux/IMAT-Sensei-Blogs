Why Cut-offs Are Never a Fixed Target

Cut-offs shift dramatically every year based on three variables: how many students sat the exam, how hard the paper was, and how many seats were available. Bari's non-EU cut-off was 31.2 in 2023, jumped to 65.8 in 2024, then dropped to 50.9 in 2025. A student using 2023 as a planning figure for 2024 did not get in anywhere. The correct approach: look at the 3-year trend, add a 3–5 point safety buffer, and prepare to reach that number.

⚠️ Never plan around a single year's cut-off. The 2024 exam was unusually easy, producing record-high scores across all universities. The 2025 exam corrected significantly. Always plan for moderate-to-hard difficulty and add a buffer above the most recent figure.

What is the DSU?

"Diritto allo Studio Universitario" — Italy's Right to University Study programme. Every Italian region funds a scholarship body that provides income-eligible students with free tuition, a cash stipend, free housing, and free meals. For IMAT students this is the single most important financial tool available — and no minimum IMAT score is required in your first year. Any enrolled student who meets the income threshold can apply.

What the DSU Covers

BenefitAmount / Detail
Tuition Waiver100% of tuition — you pay only the €156 regional tax (tassa regionale). Saves €1,000–€3,000/year.
Cash Stipend€2,400–€7,000 per year depending on student status and ISEE value
University HousingFree bed in university dormitory — scholarship holders receive priority placement
University MealsFree or heavily subsidised mensa (canteen) access — typically 1–3 free meals per day
TransportSome regions (notably Turin/EDISU Piemonte) include public transport discounts

How Much Will You Receive? Student Status Tiers

StatusConditionAnnual Stipend
On-Site (Sede)Within same municipality or under 40km / 45 min by public transport~€2,400–€3,000
Commuter (Pendolare)40–80km from university~€3,500–€4,500
Off-Site (Fuori Sede) ★More than 80km from home, renting near university~€5,000–€7,000
✅ Almost every international IMAT student qualifies as Fuori Sede. You come from abroad — automatically over 80km away. Upload your Italian rental contract to your scholarship portal to confirm this status. This is the difference between €2,400 and €7,000 per year. Do not skip this step.

Income Eligibility: ISEE Thresholds

ISEE Parificato ValueOutcome
Below ~€13,153Maximum grant — full stipend (up to €7,000/year) + full tuition waiver
€13,153–€26,306Partial grant — tuition waiver + reduced cash stipend
€26,306–€50,000Not eligible for DSU but entitled to reduced tuition based on income
Above ~€50,000Full tuition fees, no financial assistance from DSU

Thresholds vary slightly by region and year. Always check the current bando on your scholarship agency's website.

Year 2 Merit Requirements

In Year 1, the DSU is awarded on income alone. From Year 2, you must pass a minimum number of credits by a specified deadline — typically 20 CFU (ECTS) by late October or November of your first academic year. Failing to meet this threshold means your scholarship is revoked and you may need to repay funds already received. Take first-year exams seriously — this is not optional.

Application Steps

  1. Obtain ISEE Parificato (start March–April, takes 2–4 months)
  2. Enroll at your Italian university after IMAT results in October
  3. Find your regional scholarship agency website (EDISU, ER.GO, LazioDisco, ADiSUrC, etc.)
  4. Submit online DSU application before agency deadline (typically September–October each year)
  5. Upload Italian rental contract to confirm Fuori Sede status
  6. Await assessment — stipend is paid in 2–3 instalments throughout the academic year
⚠️ The ISEE Parificato is the #1 reason students miss the scholarship deadline. It takes 2–4 months to obtain. Start in March or April every year without exception. See the dedicated ISEE Parificato guide for the full step-by-step process. Turin is the only exception — EDISU Piemonte handles it directly.

Deep Notes: Cellular Respiration

This single topic accounted for 30% of biology in 2025. Master every stage in detail:

  • Glycolysis (cytoplasm): 1 glucose → 2 pyruvate + 2 ATP net + 2 NADH. 10 steps. Key enzymes: hexokinase, phosphofructokinase (rate-limiting), pyruvate kinase.
  • Pyruvate Decarboxylation (matrix): Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA + CO₂ + NADH. Pyruvate dehydrogenase complex. Requires NAD⁺, CoA, thiamine pyrophosphate.
  • Krebs / Citric Acid Cycle (matrix): 8 steps. Per turn: 3 NADH, 1 FADH₂, 1 GTP, 2 CO₂. Two turns per glucose. Key intermediates: citrate → isocitrate → α-ketoglutarate → succinyl-CoA → succinate → fumarate → malate → oxaloacetate.
  • Oxidative Phosphorylation / ETC (inner membrane): NADH feeds Complex I (NADH dehydrogenase). FADH₂ feeds Complex II (succinate dehydrogenase). Electrons flow via ubiquinone to Complex III (cytochrome bc1) → cytochrome c → Complex IV (cytochrome c oxidase) → O₂ + H⁺ → H₂O. Proton gradient across inner membrane drives ATP synthase (Complex V).
  • Total ATP yield: ~36–38 ATP per glucose. Glycolysis: 2, Krebs: 2, ETC: ~32–34.
  • Fermentation: Anaerobic. Regenerates NAD⁺. Lactic acid fermentation (animal muscle, RBCs): pyruvate → lactate (lactate dehydrogenase). Alcoholic fermentation (yeast): pyruvate → acetaldehyde → ethanol + CO₂. Both yield only 2 net ATP.
  • Chemiosmosis: Mitchell's hypothesis. Proton motive force (pmf) = proton gradient + membrane potential. ATP synthase uses H⁺ flow from IMS to matrix to phosphorylate ADP + Pi → ATP.

Deep Notes: Genetics & Inheritance

  • Mendelian genetics: Dominant alleles mask recessive. Punnett squares for mono- and dihybrid crosses. Test cross: suspected dominant × homozygous recessive. Law of Segregation, Law of Independent Assortment (Mendel's Laws).
  • Incomplete dominance: Heterozygote shows intermediate phenotype (RR red × WW white = RW pink).
  • Codominance: Both alleles expressed simultaneously (ABO blood group AB).
  • Sex-linked inheritance: X-linked recessive (haemophilia A, red-green colour blindness). Affected males: XᴬY. Carrier females: XᴬXᵃ. Pedigree analysis: identify obligate carriers, calculate risk probabilities.
  • Chromosomal disorders: Non-disjunction in meiosis → trisomy (Down = trisomy 21, Edwards = trisomy 18, Patau = trisomy 13), monosomy (Turner = 45,X). Klinefelter = 47,XXY (non-disjunction in meiosis II).

Deep Notes: Cell Organelles

  • Nucleus: Double membrane with pores. Contains chromatin (DNA + histones). Nucleolus synthesises rRNA and assembles ribosome subunits.
  • Mitochondria: Double membrane. Outer (smooth), inner (cristae — highly folded). Matrix contains: Krebs cycle enzymes, mitochondrial DNA (circular), ribosomes (70S), pyruvate decarboxylation machinery. Inner membrane: ETC complexes + ATP synthase. Endosymbiotic origin from α-proteobacteria.
  • Ribosomes: Eukaryote: 80S (60S + 40S). Prokaryote/mitochondria/chloroplast: 70S (50S + 30S). Antibiotic targets (aminoglycosides, tetracyclines target 70S — do not affect 80S).
  • Rough ER: Studded with ribosomes. Site of synthesis and initial glycosylation of secretory and membrane-bound proteins.
  • Smooth ER: No ribosomes. Lipid synthesis (phospholipids, steroids), detoxification (cytochrome P450 enzymes), calcium ion storage (muscle SR).
  • Golgi apparatus: Stack of cisternae. cis face (receives vesicles from ER), trans face (dispatches). Post-translational modification (further glycosylation, phosphorylation), sorting, packaging into vesicles for secretion, lysosomes, or membrane.
  • Lysosomes: Acid hydrolases (pH ~4.8). Degrade engulfed bacteria (phagolysosomes), worn-out organelles (autolysosomes). Lysosomal storage diseases (Tay-Sachs, Gaucher) = enzyme deficiency.
  • Chloroplasts (plants only): Double membrane. Thylakoids (stacked = grana) contain photosystems I and II, light-dependent reactions, electron transport, chemiosmosis. Stroma: Calvin cycle (CO₂ fixation → G3P). Own circular DNA — endosymbiotic origin from cyanobacteria.

Deep Notes: Enzymes

  • Active site specificity: Lock-and-key model (rigid, specific) vs induced-fit model (conformational change upon substrate binding — more accurate).
  • Michaelis-Menten: v = Vmax[S] / (Km + [S]). Vmax = maximum rate (all active sites occupied). Km = [S] at half-Vmax — inversely related to enzyme affinity. Low Km → high affinity.
  • Competitive inhibition: Inhibitor structurally resembles substrate. Binds active site reversibly. Km increases (apparent), Vmax unchanged. Overcome by excess substrate. Example: methotrexate inhibits dihydrofolate reductase.
  • Non-competitive inhibition: Inhibitor binds allosteric site (not active site). Causes conformational change reducing catalytic efficiency. Vmax decreases, Km unchanged. Cannot be overcome by excess substrate. Example: cyanide inhibits Complex IV.
  • Uncompetitive inhibition: Inhibitor binds only to enzyme-substrate complex. Both Vmax and Km decrease by same factor. Lineweaver-Burk: parallel lines.
  • Irreversible inhibition: Covalent modification of active site. Permanent inactivation. Example: aspirin acetylates COX enzymes (inhibits prostaglandin synthesis).
  • Allosteric regulation: Regulatory molecule binds allosteric site → activates or inhibits. Positive cooperativity (haemoglobin): binding of one substrate increases affinity for subsequent substrates → sigmoidal v vs [S] curve.
✅ Study tip: Draw every diagram (Krebs cycle, ETC, Punnett squares, organelle maps) from memory, not from the textbook. The act of reconstruction forces deeper encoding than passive re-reading. Then immediately do past paper questions topic-by-topic to identify gaps.

MAECI Government Scholarship: Up to €12,000/year for Non-EU Students

Italy's Ministry of Foreign Affairs awards a competitive annual scholarship to international students. Here is every detail.

What is MAECI?

MAECI (Ministero degli Affari Esteri e della Cooperazione Internazionale) is Italy's Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Each year it publishes a call (bando) offering a limited number of government-funded scholarships to international students at Italian public universities. For medicine students who qualify, this covers living costs on top of any DSU support you receive.

ℹ️ EU citizens and students already holding Italian residence are not eligible for MAECI. EU students should focus on the DSU regional scholarship. Non-EU students should pursue both — DSU first (most reliable), MAECI as a bonus if competitive.

What MAECI Covers

BenefitTypical Amount
Monthly Allowance~€900–€1,000/month for the full scholarship duration
Health InsuranceFull coverage throughout the scholarship period
Tuition ContributionVaries by call — some awards include partial or full tuition waiver
AccommodationSome awards include housing contribution or university accommodation placement

How to Apply

  1. Check the annual bando at esteri.it/en/opportunita/borse-di-studio — typically published January to March
  2. Apply through the Italian Embassy or Consulate in your home country
  3. Prepare: academic transcripts, certified diploma, motivation letter, language certificate, passport copies
  4. Submit by the Embassy's stated deadline — highly competitive, strong academic records essential

Non-EU Students: The Complete Month-by-Month Timeline — Pre-Enrollment to Permesso

One missed step = one lost year. Here is the full sequence from March to November — every platform, deadline, and document explained.

⚠️ Non-EU students who begin this process in June or July are already late for several steps. Start in March–April at the absolute latest. Begin immediately if you haven't already.

Why This Timeline Is Non-Negotiable

EU students can register for the IMAT, sit the exam, see their score, then enroll at any of their 13 ranked universities. Non-EU students must begin a completely different process 5–6 months before the exam, with hard deadlines at each stage. Missing any step pushes your entire start date back by a full academic year.

Pre-Enrollment: The Binding Choice

On Universitaly, non-EU students must pre-enroll at exactly one university before the IMAT. This is a binding, non-changeable decision. You will need: your passport, a passport photo, and your chosen university name. Your high school diploma with certified Italian translation will be required at the Embassy stage. Choose very carefully — this is the only non-EU ranking list you will appear on.

Visa D Application

After receiving your admission letter and enrollment confirmation, apply for a Type D Long-Stay Student Visa at the nearest Italian Embassy or Consulate. Typical required documents: university admission/enrollment letter, proof of financial means (DSU scholarship award letter or bank statement), accommodation proof in Italy, valid passport, 2 passport photos. Processing times vary by country from 2 weeks to 2 months — apply immediately after admission is confirmed.

Permesso di Soggiorno

Within 8 working days of arriving in Italy, go to an authorised post office (Ufficio Postale) and collect the Kit Postale — the official package of forms for the residence permit application. Complete all forms, attach copies of your passport, visa, accommodation proof, and submit. You receive a receipt (ricevuta) which acts as a temporary permit while your application is processed. You will then be called to the local Questura for biometrics within several weeks. Keep every document — the Permesso is required for banking, health insurance, scholarship registration, and everything else in Italy.

✅ Parificati strategy: Once you hold a valid Permesso di Soggiorno for one full year, you become eligible to compete as a Parificati student — effectively an EU student — in the following year's IMAT. This means ranking up to 13 universities and participating in scrolling. Students who narrowly miss their non-EU target often maintain their permit through a language school or preparatory course and retake the IMAT the following year with full EU-equivalent access.

Chemistry

IMAT Chemistry: Deep Notes on Every High-Yield Topic — Calculations and Theory

15 questions, 22.5 points. The style changes year to year. You must master both — here is exactly what to study.

The Core Problem: Style Unpredictability

In 2024, stoichiometry dominated — 7 of 15 chemistry questions were calculation-based. In 2025, the paper flipped completely to theory — bonding, redox, acids/bases, biological molecules. You cannot predict which style 2026 will use. The only solution is genuine competence in both.

Always High-Yield (Both Years, Every Style)

Acids, Bases & pH

  • pH = −log[H⁺]. pOH = −log[OH⁻]. pH + pOH = 14 at 25°C.
  • Strong acids (HCl, HNO₃, H₂SO₄, HBr, HI, HClO₄) fully dissociate. pH = −log[acid].
  • Weak acids partially dissociate. Ka = [H⁺][A⁻]/[HA]. Henderson-Hasselbalch: pH = pKa + log([A⁻]/[HA]).
  • Neutralisation: acid + base → salt + water. At equivalence point, moles acid = moles base.
  • Indicators change colour at their pKa. Litmus (pKa ~7), phenolphthalein (pKa ~9.3), methyl orange (pKa ~3.5).
  • Buffer solutions: resist pH change when small amounts of acid or base are added. Consist of weak acid + its conjugate base (or weak base + conjugate acid).

Redox Reactions

  • OIL RIG — Oxidation Is Loss (of electrons), Reduction Is Gain.
  • Oxidation state rules: O = −2 (except peroxides: −1, OF₂: +2). H = +1 (except metal hydrides: −1). Sum in compound = 0. Sum in polyatomic ion = ion charge.
  • Balancing in acidic solution: balance atoms, balance O with H₂O, balance H with H⁺, balance charge with e⁻, multiply half-reactions to equalise electrons, add.
  • Balancing in basic solution: same as acidic, then add OH⁻ to both sides to neutralise H⁺.
  • Electrochemical cells: anode = oxidation (negative terminal in galvanic cell), cathode = reduction. Standard electrode potential E°. E°cell = E°cathode − E°anode. Positive E°cell = spontaneous reaction.

Gas Laws

  • Boyle's: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂ (constant T, n)
  • Charles's: V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂ (constant P, n). Temperature must be in Kelvin: K = °C + 273.15
  • Gay-Lussac's: P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂ (constant V, n)
  • Combined: P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂
  • Ideal Gas: PV = nRT. R = 8.314 J/(mol·K) or 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K). STP (0°C, 1 atm): 1 mole = 22.4 L.
  • Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures: Ptotal = P₁ + P₂ + P₃...

Chemical Equilibrium

  • Le Chatelier's Principle: system at equilibrium shifts to counteract any applied stress.
  • Adding reactant → shifts forward. Removing reactant → shifts backward. Same for products (opposite direction).
  • Increasing temperature → favours endothermic direction. Decreasing → exothermic direction.
  • Increasing pressure → favours side with fewer moles of gas.
  • Kc = [products]^n / [reactants]^m (stoichiometric coefficients as exponents). Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn where Δn = moles gas products − moles gas reactants.
  • Catalysts increase rate of both forward and reverse reactions equally — do not shift equilibrium.

Calculation-Heavy Style (2024 Pattern)

  • Mole calculations: moles = mass / molar mass. Molar mass = sum of atomic masses (g/mol). Avogadro: 6.022 × 10²³ entities/mol.
  • Stoichiometry: balance equation → identify mole ratio → convert mass to moles → use ratio → convert to required unit. Always identify limiting reagent: divide moles of each reactant by its stoichiometric coefficient — smallest value is limiting.
  • Concentration: Molarity (M) = mol/L. Dilution: C₁V₁ = C₂V₂. Molality = mol/kg solvent.
  • Percentage yield: % yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100.
  • Empirical formula from % composition: divide % by atomic mass → divide all by smallest → multiply to get whole numbers.

Theory-Heavy Style (2025 Pattern)

  • Atomic orbitals: s (spherical, 2e), p (dumbbell, 6e), d (10e), f (14e). Aufbau principle (fill lowest energy first), Hund's rule (half-fill before pairing), Pauli exclusion (no two electrons same 4 quantum numbers). Exceptions: Cr ([Ar]3d⁵4s¹), Cu ([Ar]3d¹⁰4s¹).
  • Periodic trends: Atomic radius decreases left→right (more protons), increases top→bottom. Ionisation energy and electronegativity opposite. Most electronegative: F (3.98).
  • Chemical bonding: Ionic (metal + non-metal, ΔEN > 1.7). Polar covalent (0.4 < ΔEN < 1.7). Nonpolar covalent (ΔEN < 0.4). Metallic (electron sea). Hydrogen bonds: X−H···Y where X, Y = N, O, or F. Strongest intermolecular force — responsible for water's high boiling point, surface tension, and density anomaly.
  • Molecular geometry (VSEPR): Count electron pairs around central atom. Linear (2, 180°), trigonal planar (3, 120°), tetrahedral (4, 109.5°), trigonal pyramidal (3 bonds + 1 lone pair, ~107°, e.g. NH₃), bent (2 bonds + 2 lone pairs, ~104.5°, e.g. H₂O).
  • Organic chemistry: Functional groups — alkene (C=C), alkyne (C≡C), alcohol (−OH), aldehyde (−CHO), ketone (C=O in chain), carboxylic acid (−COOH), amine (−NH₂), ester (−COO−), amide (−CONH−). IUPAC naming: longest chain = parent name, substituents as prefixes with locants.
✅ Strategy: Make one formula sheet covering every chemistry equation and memorise it in the first 2 weeks. Practice applying each formula until recall is automatic. Formula recall under exam pressure is slower than true memory — eliminate that bottleneck early.

Visa & Admin

ISEE Parificato: The Document That Unlocks Your Scholarship — Start in March or April

The #1 reason IMAT students miss their scholarship deadline, and it is 100% avoidable if you start on time.

What Is the ISEE Parificato?

ISEE (Indicatore della Situazione Economica Equivalente) is Italy's official household income assessment. For international students whose family earns income abroad, you need the ISEE Parificato — a special version requiring official income documentation from your home country, certified translation into Italian, and processing through an Italian CAF (tax assistance centre). This single value determines whether you receive the DSU scholarship and how much you get.

⚠️ The ISEE Parificato takes 2–4 months to obtain. Students who begin in June or July routinely miss the October–November scholarship deadline. Start in March or April every year, without exception. This is not optional.

The 5-Step Process

  1. Obtain official income declarations from your home country's tax authority — covering the previous calendar year (e.g. for a 2026/27 scholarship, you need 2024 income). Examples: FBR Tax Return (Pakistan), ITR / Form 16 (India), SA302 (UK), IRS transcript (USA). The exact document varies by country — check with the Italian Embassy in your country for the specific requirement.
  2. Apostille or legalise the documents — if your country is a signatory of the Hague Convention (most countries are), an apostille stamp suffices. If not, you need full legalisation through the Italian Embassy in your country. Check hcch.net for your country's status. This step alone can take 2–6 weeks.
  3. Certified Italian translation — all foreign-language documents must be translated by a sworn/certified translator (traduttore giurato). Not a simple translation service — it must be officially certified. Typical cost €30–€80 per document.
  4. Submit to a CAF in Italy — once in Italy (or through a trusted representative already there), bring all originals and certified translations to a CAF (Centro di Assistenza Fiscale). They process your DSU declaration and calculate your ISEE value. This can take 4–6 weeks during peak season (September–November). Book an appointment early.
  5. Upload ISEE certificate to your scholarship portal — once received, upload it to your university's regional scholarship agency portal before their deadline. This step is how your scholarship application is formally assessed.

ISEE Thresholds

ISEE Parificato ValueOutcome

€13,153 – €26,306Partial grant — full tuition waiver + reduced stipend
€26,306 – €50,000No DSU grant — entitled to reduced tuition based on income bracket

Thresholds vary slightly by region and academic year. Always check the current bando on your scholarship agency's website.

✅ The Turin exception: EDISU Piemonte (University of Turin) is the only regional scholarship agency in Italy that calculates the ISEE Parificato directly for applicants. You do not need to independently visit a CAF. This alone makes Turin the most bureaucratically simple choice for first-time non-EU scholarship applicants.

IMAT Scoring Strategy: The Maths Behind When to Answer, Skip, or Guess

+1.5 correct, −0.4 wrong, 0 blank. Once you understand this, exam anxiety becomes a calculated edge.

The Mathematics of Guessing

Every IMAT question has exactly 5 answer options. The scoring is: +1.5 for correct, −0.4 for wrong, 0 for blank. This means the decision to guess or skip is mathematically significant.

Pure random guess (no elimination): EV = (1/5 × 1.5) + (4/5 × −0.4) = 0.30 − 0.32 = −0.02 — marginally negative. Not worth it.

Eliminate 1 option, guess from 4: EV = (1/4 × 1.5) + (3/4 × −0.4) = 0.375 − 0.30 = +0.075 — now positive. Always guess.

Eliminate 2 options, guess from 3: EV = (1/3 × 1.5) + (2/3 × −0.4) = 0.50 − 0.267 = +0.233 — strongly worth guessing.

✅ The rule: If you can confidently eliminate at least 1 option, always guess. Only leave completely blank if you have genuinely zero basis for eliminating any option at all. In practice, most questions allow elimination of 1–2 wrong options even with partial knowledge.

EU vs Non-EU: Two Completely Separate Competitions — How Italian Admissions Actually Works

The most misunderstood structural fact about IMAT. Once you understand this, your entire strategy changes.

The Fundamental Rule

EU and non-EU students compete on entirely separate ranking lists and never compete against each other for the same seats. A high-scoring non-EU student does not take a seat away from an EU student. They are completely parallel processes with separate seat pools, separate platforms, and different strategic requirements.

Who Counts as EU?

Citizens of all 27 EU member states, plus: Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein (EEA), Switzerland, San Marino, Vatican City. Additionally, Parificati — non-EU nationals who hold a valid Italian Permesso di Soggiorno — are treated as EU students for IMAT ranking purposes.

The Scrolling System (EU Only)

EU rankings are released in multiple rounds over several weeks after the initial October release. When a student accepts an offer at their first-choice university and declines others, or when students decide not to enroll, their unclaimed seats roll down to the next eligible candidate in the ranking. This "scorrimento" (scrolling) process can continue for 10 or more rounds, with the effective cut-off dropping in each round.

This means EU students whose score initially appears just below the cut-off can still receive an offer in a later round — sometimes weeks after the initial release. Always stay enrolled in the Universitaly system and monitor the rankings during this period.

The Parificati Strategy for Non-EU Students

Non-EU students who obtain an Italian Permesso di Soggiorno and maintain it for one full year become eligible to compete as Parificati — effectively EU students — in the following year's IMAT. This means ranking up to 13 universities, participating in all scrolling rounds, and having a dramatically higher statistical chance of admission. To maintain the permit you must be enrolled in an official study programme (language school, preparatory course, or another university programme) — simply staying in Italy without a valid academic reason does not count.

Physics & Maths

IMAT Physics & Maths: All 13 Questions Covered — Notes, Formulas & Priority Order

13 questions, 19.5 points. The sections most students under-prepare. Here is everything you need.

Physics (6 Questions)

Electricity & Circuits ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Always Appears

  • Ohm's Law: V = IR (Voltage = Current × Resistance). V in volts, I in amperes, R in ohms.
  • Series circuits: Rₜ = R₁ + R₂ + R₃. Current same through all. Voltage divides proportionally.
  • Parallel circuits: 1/Rₜ = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃. Voltage same across all branches. Current divides.
  • Power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R. Power in watts.
  • Capacitors: Q = CV (charge = capacitance × voltage). In parallel: Cₜ = C₁ + C₂. In series: 1/Cₜ = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂.
  • Electric field: E = F/q. Coulomb's Law: F = kq₁q₂/r². k = 8.99 × 10⁹ N·m²/C².
  • Electric potential: V = kq/r. Work done moving charge: W = qV.

Kinematics ⭐⭐⭐⭐

  • SUVAT: v = u + at | s = ut + ½at² | v² = u² + 2as | s = (u+v)t/2
  • Projectile motion: horizontal (constant velocity: x = v₀ₓt) and vertical (constant acceleration g = 9.8 m/s²) are independent. At max height, vᵧ = 0.
  • Free fall: v = gt, s = ½gt², time to fall height h: t = √(2h/g).

Newton's Laws & Friction ⭐⭐⭐⭐

  • 1st: object maintains state unless net force acts. 2nd: ΣF = ma. 3rd: every action has equal and opposite reaction.
  • Friction: f = μN. Static friction coefficient > kinetic friction coefficient.
  • Inclined plane: component parallel to slope = mg sinθ (friction force), perpendicular = mg cosθ (normal force).

Thermodynamics ⭐⭐⭐ — Absent 2025, study anyway

  • 1st Law: ΔU = Q − W. Internal energy change = heat added − work done by system.
  • Specific heat: Q = mcΔT. Latent heat: Q = mL (phase changes, T constant).
  • Ideal gas: PV = nRT. Internal energy of monatomic ideal gas: U = (3/2)nRT.
  • Heat transfer: conduction (through material, ΔT drives flow), convection (fluid movement), radiation (electromagnetic waves, no medium needed).

Gravitation & Fluids ⭐⭐⭐

  • Gravitational force: F = Gm₁m₂/r². G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg². g = GM/r².
  • Fluid pressure: P = P₀ + ρgh. Pressure increases with depth.
  • Archimedes: buoyant force = weight of displaced fluid. Object floats if ρ_object < ρ_fluid.
  • Bernoulli: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant. Faster fluid → lower pressure.

Mathematics (7 Questions)

Algebra & Equations ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Core, Always Appears

  • Quadratic formula: x = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) / 2a. Discriminant: b²−4ac > 0 (two real roots), = 0 (one), < 0 (no real roots).
  • Systems of equations: substitution and elimination methods.
  • Polynomial and rational inequalities: factor, find roots, test sign in each interval.
  • Absolute value: |x| < a ↔ −a < x < a. |x| > a ↔ x < −a or x > a.

Logarithms & Exponents ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Absent 2025, study anyway

  • log_a(x) = y ↔ aʸ = x. ln(x) = log_e(x). log(xy) = log x + log y. log(x/y) = log x − log y. log(xⁿ) = n log x. log_a(a) = 1. log_a(1) = 0.
  • Exponential equations: take log of both sides, isolate exponent.

Probability ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Absent 2025, study anyway

  • P(A) = favourable / total. P(A and B) = P(A)·P(B) for independent events. P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A and B).
  • Permutations: nPr = n!/(n−r)!. Combinations: nCr = n!/[r!(n−r)!].
  • Conditional probability: P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B).

Geometry & Trigonometry ⭐⭐⭐

  • Circle: area = πr², circumference = 2πr. Sector area = (θ/360)πr².
  • Pythagoras: a² + b² = c². Triangle area = ½bh. Similar triangles: ratios of sides equal.
  • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1. tan θ = sin θ / cos θ. Key values: sin 30° = 0.5, cos 30° = √3/2, sin 45° = cos 45° = √2/2, sin 60° = √3/2, cos 60° = 0.5.
  • Coordinate geometry: distance = √((x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²). Midpoint = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2). Line: y = mx + c, gradient m = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁).
⚠️ Logarithms, exponents, probability, and thermodynamics were all absent in 2025 but appeared in 2023 and earlier. Do not skip these topics — they are likely to return in 2026.
