Why Cut-offs Are Not a Fixed Target

Cut-offs shift every single year based on how many students applied, how hard the exam was, and how many seats were available. Bari's non-EU cut-off was 31.2 in 2023, jumped to 65.8 in 2024, then fell back to 50.9 in 2025. A student who used 2023 as a target in 2024 did not get in. The correct approach: pick your target cities, find the relevant cut-offs across multiple years, add a 3–5 point buffer, and build your preparation toward that number.

⚠️ Never use a single year's cut-off as your target. Plan for a competitive paper, not the easiest one in recent history.

DSU Scholarship: Free Tuition + Up to €7,000 Cash Per Year

Italy's regional scholarship is the most powerful financial opportunity for IMAT students. Here is everything you need to know — completely free.

What is the DSU?

"Diritto allo Studio Universitario" — the Right to University Study. Every Italian region runs a welfare system that provides income-eligible students with free or subsidised education. For medicine students this is far and away the most valuable scholarship available. It does not require a minimum IMAT score. Any enrolled student who meets the income threshold can apply in Year 1.

What It Covers

BenefitDetail
Tuition Waiver100% — you pay only the €156 regional tax (tassa regionale). Saves €1,000–€3,000/year.
Cash Stipend€2,400–€7,000/year depending on student status and ISEE value
University HousingFree bed in a university dorm (priority to scholarship holders)
Free MealsUniversity canteen (mensa) access — free or heavily subsidised
TransportSome regions (e.g. Turin/EDISU) include public transport discounts

How Much Will You Get?

The amount depends on your student status — how far you live from the university.

StatusConditionAnnual Stipend
On-Site (Sede)Same city, within 40km/40min~€2,400–€3,000
Commuter (Pendolare)40–80km away, daily commute~€3,500–€4,500
Off-Site (Fuori Sede)Over 80km, renting near university~€5,000–€7,000
✅ Most international students qualify as Fuori Sede because they come from abroad — automatically giving you the highest stipend tier. Upload your rent contract to the scholarship portal to confirm this. Do not skip this step.

Income Thresholds (ISEE Parificato)

  • ISEE below ~€13,153: Maximum grant — full stipend + full tuition waiver
  • ISEE €13,153–€26,306: Partial grant — tuition waiver + reduced stipend
  • ISEE above ~€26,306: Not eligible for DSU scholarship

Exact thresholds vary slightly by region and year. Always check the current year's bando on your university's scholarship portal.

Year 2 Merit Requirement

From Year 2 onwards, you must have passed at least 20 CFU (ECTS credits) by the November deadline of your first year. Miss this threshold and your scholarship is revoked — and you may be required to repay funds already received. Make passing first-year exams a priority.

The Critical Document: ISEE Parificato

International students need a special income assessment called the ISEE Parificato. It requires official income documents from your home country, certified translations, and submission to a CAF (tax assistance centre) in Italy. The process typically takes 2–4 months.

⚠️ Start the ISEE Parificato process in March or April every year. Missing the scholarship deadline because your ISEE wasn't ready in time is the single most common and avoidable mistake IMAT students make. Turin is an exception — EDISU Piedmont calculates it directly, removing this step.

Hidden Gems Worth Serious Consideration

Parma — 45 non-EU seats, one of the highest allocations in Italy. ER.GO covers both Parma and Bologna with the same application. Lower competition than Bologna itself, cheaper city, very popular with international students.

IMAT Biology Deep-Dive: What Dominated 2025 and What to Prioritise for 2026

23 questions. 34.5 points maximum. The single highest-leverage section in the exam.

Why Biology Is Your Most Important Subject

Biology carries 38% of all available points. Getting biology right is the biggest single lever on your final score. The IMAT writers since 2023 have concentrated questions heavily on 1–2 topics per year, meaning surface-level coverage of everything is not enough — you need deep understanding of the high-yield areas.

Your Priority Study List for 2026

  • Cellular Respiration — glycolysis, Krebs cycle, oxidative phosphorylation, chemiosmosis, ATP yield, fermentation. Go deep. This can be 30% of biology again.
  • Cell Biology & Organelles — structure and function of every organelle, differences between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells, plant vs animal cells.
  • Genetics & Inheritance — Mendelian genetics, co-dominance, sex-linkage, pedigree analysis, chromosomal disorders.
  • Central Dogma (DNA/RNA/Protein) — DNA replication, transcription, translation, genetic code, mutations, gene regulation.
  • Enzymes — enzyme kinetics, Michaelis-Menten, inhibition types, cofactors, activation energy, pH and temperature effects.
  • Cell Membranes & Transport — fluid mosaic model, passive and active transport, osmosis, endocytosis/exocytosis.
  • Muscles & Physiology — sliding filament model, neuromuscular junction, muscle fibre types, cardiac vs skeletal muscle.
  • Biological Molecules — structure and function of carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, nucleic acids.

MAECI Italian Government Scholarship — Complete Guide for Non-EU Students

A competitive national scholarship from Italy's Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Open to non-EU students only.

What is the MAECI Scholarship?

MAECI (Ministero degli Affari Esteri e della Cooperazione Internazionale) is Italy's Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Each year it publishes a call (bando) offering a limited number of scholarships to international students for study at Italian universities. For medicine students, this can significantly reduce living costs on top of any DSU scholarship you receive.

ℹ️ EU students and students already residing in Italy are not eligible for MAECI. EU students should focus on the DSU regional scholarship. Non-EU students should pursue both — DSU first (most reliable), MAECI as a bonus.

What It Covers

  • Monthly living allowance (amount varies by call — typically €900–€1,000/month)
  • Health insurance coverage for the duration
  • In some cases, contribution toward tuition fees
  • Some awards include accommodation assistance

How to Apply

  • Check the annual call (bando) at: esteri.it/en/opportunita/borse-di-studio
  • Applications typically open January–March for the following academic year
  • Apply through the Italian Embassy or Consulate in your home country
  • Required documents: academic transcripts, motivation letter, passport, language certificate (varies by country)
  • Selection is competitive — strong academic records are essential

MAECI vs DSU — Which to Prioritise?

Non-EU Students: The Exact Bureaucratic Timeline From Pre-Enrollment to Visa D

Missing a single deadline can cost you an entire year. Here is exactly what to do and when.

The Critical Difference: EU vs Non-EU Process

EU students have a streamlined path — rank multiple universities, no visa needed, enroll after results. Non-EU students face a multi-step bureaucratic sequence with hard, non-negotiable deadlines. This process typically begins 5–6 months before the September exam.

⚠️ Non-EU students who start this process in June or July are already too late for several steps. Begin in March–April at the latest.

Pre-Enrollment: The Binding University Choice

Non-EU students must pre-enroll at exactly one university on Universitaly before the IMAT. This is a binding decision. You need your passport, a recent passport photo, and your chosen university. Your high school diploma (with certified translation) will be required later. Choose carefully — your pre-enrollment university is the only place you can be admitted as non-EU.

Visa D (Long-Stay Student Visa)

After you receive your admission letter and enrollment confirmation from the Italian university, apply for a Type D Student Visa at your nearest Italian Embassy or Consulate. Required documents typically include: acceptance letter, proof of enrollment, proof of financial means (scholarship letter or bank statement), accommodation confirmation, and a valid passport. Processing times vary by country — start immediately after receiving admission.

Permesso di Soggiorno (Residence Permit)

Within 8 working days of arriving in Italy, you must apply for your Permesso di Soggiorno at an Italian post office (Ufficio Postale) using the official kit forms (Kit Postale). Bring your passport, visa, completed kit forms, accommodation proof, and passport photos. You will then be called to your local Questura (police headquarters) for biometrics. This permit is essential for everything in Italy — banking, health insurance, scholarship registration, and more.

✅ Parificati strategy: Non-EU students who obtain a Permesso di Soggiorno and maintain it for one full year become eligible to apply as EU students (Parificati) the following year — allowing them to rank multiple universities. This is a well-known approach for students who narrowly miss their target on the first attempt.

The 15-Week IMAT Study Plan: Phase-by-Phase From Foundation to Exam Day

Never wonder what to study next. Here is the complete structure from first week to exam week.

Why Your Start Date Matters More Than You Think

Students who begin preparing by May score significantly higher than those who start in July or August. Past papers only become useful once you have a content foundation to draw on — starting late means you never get the full value from practice. The syllabus is larger than it looks at first glance.

Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–6 | ~May–June)

Goal: cover the full syllabus content. No past papers yet — build the knowledge base first.

  • Week 1–2: Biology — Cell Biology, Organelles, Membranes, Transport, Biological Molecules
  • Week 3–4: Biology — Cellular Respiration (allocate extra time here), Enzymes, Genetics, Central Dogma
  • Week 5: Chemistry — Atomic structure, Periodic trends, Chemical Bonding
  • Week 6: Chemistry — Acids & Bases, Redox Reactions, Gas Laws, Stoichiometry

Phase 2 — Build (Weeks 7–10 | ~July)

Goal: complete remaining syllabus and begin topic-by-topic past paper questions.

  • Week 7: Chemistry — Equilibrium, Organic Chemistry, Kinetics, Biological Molecules (chemistry side)
  • Week 8: Physics — Electricity & Circuits, Kinematics, Newton's Laws, Thermodynamics, Fluids
  • Week 9: Mathematics — Algebra, Logs & Exponents, Probability, Geometry, Trigonometry
  • Week 10: Logic & Reading — practice daily with timed reasoning sets. Read medical-style English texts to prepare for the reading section.

Phase 3 — Integration (Weeks 11–13 | ~August)

Goal: full timed mock exams under real conditions. Identify weak areas and target them.

  • One full 60-question mock per week under strict 100-minute conditions
  • After each mock: categorise every wrong answer — knowledge gap, careless error, or time pressure
  • Spend 60% of non-mock time on your three weakest identified topics
  • Review high-yield biology and chemistry summaries weekly

Phase 4 — Peak (Weeks 14–15 | September)

Goal: consolidate confidence, handle logistics, do not introduce new content.

  • One final mock in the week before the exam — no new topics
  • Review only formula cards and key biology summary sheets daily
  • Confirm your test centre location, travel, and ID documents the week before
  • In the final 3 days: rest, eat well, sleep properly. Your brain needs to perform, not absorb new information.
✅ Prioritise Biology and Chemistry in all phases. Together they account for 63% of all available points. Mastering these two subjects first and reinforcing them throughout gives you the highest return on every hour of study time.

Chemistry

IMAT Chemistry: How to Prepare for Both Calculation-Heavy and Theory-Heavy Papers

15 questions, 22.5 points. The second most important section — and the one with the most unpredictable style.

The Unpredictability Problem

In 2024, stoichiometry and calculation problems dominated chemistry — 7 of 15 questions were numerical. In 2025, the paper flipped completely to theory — redox, bonds, and acids/bases each had 2 questions, with no heavy calculation problems. You cannot know which style 2026 will use. You must prepare for both.

Always High-Yield (Every Year, Every Style)

  • Acids & Bases / pH — appeared in 2024 and 2025. pH calculations, buffer solutions, acid-base indicators, strong vs weak acids
  • Redox Reactions — oxidation states, half-reactions, balancing redox equations, electrochemical cells
  • Gas Laws — Boyle's, Charles's, Gay-Lussac's, Ideal Gas, combined numerical problems
  • Chemical Equilibrium — Le Chatelier's principle, Kc and Kp expressions, effect of temperature and pressure

When the Paper Is Calculation-Heavy (2024 Style)

  • Mole concept and molar mass calculations
  • Limiting reagent and theoretical yield problems
  • Solution concentration — molarity, molality, percentage concentration
  • Stoichiometry across states (solid, liquid, gas)
  • Dilution problems and solution mixing

When the Paper Is Theory-Heavy (2025 Style)

  • Types of chemical bonds — ionic, covalent, metallic, hydrogen bonding
  • Molecular geometry (VSEPR), polarity, dipole moments
  • Periodic table trends — electronegativity, ionisation energy, atomic radius
  • Atomic orbitals, electron configuration, quantum numbers
  • Reaction kinetics — rate laws, activation energy, catalysts
  • Organic chemistry — functional groups, naming, basic reaction types
⚠️ Students who only practise calculations, or only study theory, are gambling on which style the paper will use. Both must be prepared. The only way to guarantee points regardless of the paper is to be competent in both.

Visa & Admin

ISEE Parificato: The Document That Unlocks Your Scholarship — Start 4 Months Early

The single most commonly missed scholarship deadline — and it is 100% avoidable if you start in time.

What Is the ISEE Parificato?

ISEE stands for "Indicatore della Situazione Economica Equivalente" — Italy's system for assessing your family's economic status. For Italian residents, this is a standard process. For international students whose family earns income abroad, you need the ISEE Parificato — a more complex version that requires documents from your home country to be certified and assessed.

This single number (your ISEE value) determines whether you receive the DSU scholarship and how much cash stipend you get. Without it, you cannot apply.

⚠️ The ISEE Parificato is the #1 reason IMAT students miss their scholarship. The process takes 2–4 months. Start in March or April every year without exception.

Step-by-Step Process

  • Step 1: Obtain official income and asset declarations from your home country's tax authority, covering the previous calendar year (January–December). For most students in 2026, this means income data from 2024.
  • Step 2: Have documents apostilled or legalised — the exact requirement depends on your country's treaty status with Italy. Apostille countries use an apostille stamp. Non-apostille countries need full legalisation through the Italian Embassy.
  • Step 3: Get all documents translated into Italian by a certified translator (traduttore giurato). Self-translations are not accepted.
  • Step 4: Once in Italy (or through a representative), bring everything to a CAF (Centro di Assistenza Fiscale — a tax assistance centre). They complete the DSU declaration form and calculate your ISEE value. This can take 2–6 weeks during peak season.
  • Step 5: Submit the ISEE certificate to your university's scholarship portal before the deadline (typically September–November).
✅ Turin exception: EDISU Piedmont (University of Turin's scholarship agency) calculates the ISEE Parificato directly for applicants. You do not need to go through a CAF independently. This removes one of the most stressful steps and makes Turin an especially student-friendly choice for non-EU applicants who want to reduce admin burden.

IMAT Scoring Maths: When to Answer, When to Skip, When to Guess

Understanding this turns a nervous habit into a calculated edge.

The Scoring System

Every IMAT question is multiple choice with 5 options. Scoring: +1.5 correct, −0.4 wrong, 0 blank. Maximum possible score: 90 points.

When Is Guessing Worth It?

Random guessing (no elimination): Expected value = (1/5 × 1.5) + (4/5 × −0.4) = 0.3 − 0.32 = −0.02. Marginally negative — not worth it.

After eliminating 1 option (guess from 4): Expected value = (1/4 × 1.5) + (3/4 × −0.4) = 0.375 − 0.3 = +0.075. Now guessing is mathematically positive.

After eliminating 2 options (guess from 3): Expected value = (1/3 × 1.5) + (2/3 × −0.4) = 0.5 − 0.267 = +0.233. Strongly worth guessing.

✅ The rule: If you can eliminate at least 1 answer option, always guess. If you truly have zero ability to narrow it down, leave it blank. Most questions allow elimination of at least 1–2 obviously wrong options.

  • Pass 1: Answer everything you know confidently and quickly — aim to complete this in 55–60 minutes

  • Section priority: Biology (35 min) → Chemistry (25 min) → Physics (8 min) → Maths (10 min) → Logic+Reading (22 min)

EU vs Non-EU: Two Completely Separate Competitions — How It Actually Works

The most misunderstood structural fact about IMAT admissions — and why it changes your strategy entirely.

The Core Rule

EU and non-EU students compete on entirely separate ranking lists. You never compete against the other group. Seats are allocated separately from separate pools. A high-scoring non-EU student does not take a seat from an EU student, and vice versa.

Who Counts as EU?

EU citizens (all 27 EU member states) plus: Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, Switzerland, San Marino, and Vatican City. Additionally, Parificati — non-EU students who hold a valid Italian Permesso di Soggiorno (residence permit) — are treated as EU students for IMAT purposes.

The EU Scrolling Advantage

EU students participate in multiple rounds of seat redistribution ("scorrimento"). If you don't get your first-choice university, you automatically stay in the ranking for your second, third choice, and so on as seats free up. This dramatically increases the chances of getting a seat somewhere. The process can continue for several months after the initial rankings are published.

The Parificati Strategy

Non-EU students who obtain an Italian residence permit and maintain it for one full year become eligible to compete as EU students (Parificati) the following year. This means ranking up to 13 universities instead of one, and participating in scrolling. Students who narrowly miss their target on the first attempt often use the year to study Italian at a language school or enroll in a science undergraduate programme to maintain their permit — and re-take the IMAT the following year with full EU-equivalent access.
